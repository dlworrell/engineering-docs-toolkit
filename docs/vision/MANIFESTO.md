# The Semantic Document Engineering Manifesto

## Documents are knowledge

Documents are not merely collections of pages. They are collections of semantic relationships expressed through language, mathematics, diagrams, references, evidence, and structure.

Pages are one presentation of that knowledge. They are not the knowledge itself.

## Presentation changes

Fonts change. Page sizes change. Publishing technologies change. Storage media change.

Meaning should not.

EDT preserves technical knowledge by separating semantic meaning from visual presentation.

## Semantics before appearance

A heading is more than larger text.

A theorem is more than a numbered paragraph.

A figure is more than an image.

A citation is more than a hyperlink.

Engineering documents contain semantic relationships that must survive every transformation.

## Provenance is part of the document

Every published statement should remain traceable to its origin.

Every transformation should preserve the chain of evidence linking output back to source material.

Trust depends on reproducibility.

## Normalize once, publish everywhere

Importers translate source material into a canonical semantic representation.

Publishers consume that representation and render it into target formats.

The semantic core remains stable even when input and output formats change.

## Validation checks meaning

Validation is not typography.

Validation ensures that references resolve, figures have captions, theorems have proofs, accessibility requirements are respected, and semantic relationships remain intact.

## Open standards first

EDT adopts existing standards whenever they satisfy engineering requirements.

New formats or conventions should be created only when existing standards are demonstrably insufficient.

Interoperability is a design objective, not an afterthought.

## Knowledge should outlive software

Engineering manuals, specifications, research papers, and technical references often survive for decades.

EDT exists to ensure that their meaning survives as well.

## Our commitment

We build systems that preserve knowledge.

We separate semantics from presentation.

We preserve provenance.

We validate meaning.

We publish reproducibly.

We engineer for decades, not merely releases.
